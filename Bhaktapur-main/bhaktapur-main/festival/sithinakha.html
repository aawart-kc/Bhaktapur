Sithi Nakha: The Festival of Water and Renewal

Sithi Nakha is a traditional Newar festival celebrated in Bhaktapur to honor **Lord Kumar (Skanda) and the water deity**, and to promote cleanliness and renewal. The festival involves cleaning water sources, ponds, and streets, combined with religious rituals, community gatherings, and cultural performances.

1. Origin & Mythology

- Sithi Nakha is associated with **Lord Kumar**, the god of war and children, and is believed to bless the community with health, prosperity, and protection from diseases.

- The festival also honors **water spirits**, emphasizing the importance of clean water sources for agricultural and household purposes.


2. Historical Background

- The festival has been celebrated for centuries by the Newar community, dating back to the **Malla period**.

- Traditionally, it was a day for **community participation**, cleaning ponds, canals, and wells, ensuring access to safe water for the city.
