Sithi Nakha: The Festival of Water and Renewal

Sithi Nakha is a traditional Newar festival celebrated in Bhaktapur to honor **Lord Kumar (Skanda) and the water deity**, and to promote cleanliness and renewal. The festival involves cleaning water sources, ponds, and streets, combined with religious rituals, community gatherings, and cultural performances.

1. Origin & Mythology

- Sithi Nakha is associated with **Lord Kumar**, the god of war and children, and is believed to bless the community with health, prosperity, and protection from diseases.

- The festival also honors **water spirits**, emphasizing the importance of clean water sources for agricultural and household purposes.


2. Historical Background

- The festival has been celebrated for centuries by the Newar community, dating back to the **Malla period**.

- Traditionally, it was a day for **community participation**, cleaning ponds, canals, and wells, ensuring access to safe water for the city.


3. When Is It Celebrated?

- Date: Celebrated on the **6th day of the lunar month of Jestha (May–June)** according to the Nepali calendar.

- It marks the **beginning of the monsoon season**, when water sources are crucial for farming and daily life.


4. Key Locations

-Ponds, wells, and canals across Bhaktapur – cleaned and decorated by local communities.

-Temples dedicated to Lord Kumar and water deities – sites of ritual offerings and prayers.

-Neighborhood squares – community gatherings and cultural performances take place here.


5. Major Rituals and Events

💧 Cleaning of Water Sources

Communities clean **ponds, canals, wells, and public taps**, ensuring water purity and city hygiene.

🛕 Ritual Offerings

Devotees make offerings of flowers, rice, and butter lamps to Lord Kumar and local water deities to seek blessings.

🎶 Cultural Performances

Local musicians perform traditional songs, and some neighborhoods organize **mask dances and processions** to mark the occasion.


6. Cultural Significance

-Spiritual and Environmental: Combines **religious devotion with environmental responsibility**, promoting clean water and hygiene.

-Community Cohesion: Encourages neighborhoods to work together in cleaning, decorating, and performing rituals.

-Agricultural Importance: Marks the beginning of the monsoon, blessing fields and farmers for a good harvest.


7. Tourist Tips

-Participate respectfully in community cleaning and rituals.

-Visit temple areas and public squares to observe traditional offerings and cultural performances.

-Bring comfortable shoes if exploring ponds and canals, as cleaning ceremonies often involve wading into water.


8. Hidden Gems

-Some neighborhoods create **decorative water shrines** with flowers, clay figurines, and painted stones.

-Local elders often recite **traditional prayers and folklore** connected to Lord Kumar and water spirits.

-The festival offers a glimpse into **Newar environmental wisdom**, showing centuries-old practices for water conservation and urban hygiene.
