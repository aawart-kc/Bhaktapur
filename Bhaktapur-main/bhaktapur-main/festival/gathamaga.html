Gathamaga Charya: The Dance of Demons and Purification of Bhaktapur

Gathamaga Charya is a traditional Newar festival celebrated in Bhaktapur to drive away evil spirits and purify the city before the lunar New Year. The festival is marked by vibrant masked dances, musical performances, and symbolic rituals rooted deeply in local mythology and community participation.

1. Origin & Mythology

-Gathamaga Charya literally means ‚ÄúFestival of Demons‚Äù in the Newar language. According to legend, the ritual was started to protect the city from evil spirits and epidemics that threatened the people.

-The festival involves performers wearing frightening masks of demons, representing the evil forces being expelled from Bhaktapur before the New Year.


2. Historical Background

-The festival has been celebrated for centuries by the Newar community, tracing back to the Malla period. It was designed not only to drive away evil but also to strengthen social cohesion and reinforce cultural traditions.

-Gathamaga Charya was historically performed in open squares and neighborhoods, with each locality taking part in dances and rituals.


3. When Is It Celebrated?

-Date: The festival is celebrated on the **14th or 15th day of the lunar month of Falgun**, typically a few days before the Nepali New Year.

-It serves as a prelude to the New Year festivities, cleansing the city spiritually and socially.
