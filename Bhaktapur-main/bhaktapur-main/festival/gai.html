-The festival promotes the idea that laughter can be a powerful healer in times of loss.


2. Historical Background

-Gai Jatra has been celebrated for several centuries in Nepal, especially among the Newar community in Kathmandu and Lalitpur.

-It combines Hindu and Buddhist rituals with local customs and has evolved into a cultural event featuring satire, comedy shows, and public performances.


3. When Is It Celebrated?

-Gai Jatra is observed on the first day of the month of Bhadra in the Nepali calendar, usually falling in August or September.

-The festival lasts for one day, although some communities have extended celebrations with cultural programs.
