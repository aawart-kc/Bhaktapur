Yomari Purnima: The Festival of Sweet Dumplings

Yomari Purnima is a traditional Newar festival celebrated in Bhaktapur, marking the end of the rice harvest. Devotees prepare **Yomari**, a steamed dumpling filled with sweet substances like molasses or sesame seeds, and offer them to gods as a gesture of gratitude. The festival blends religious devotion, culinary tradition, and community celebration.

1. Origin & Mythology

- Yomari Purnima honors the **agricultural bounty** and thanks deities for a successful harvest.

- According to legend, offering Yomari pleases **Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth**, ensuring prosperity and abundance for the community.


2. Historical Background

- The festival has been celebrated by the Newar community for centuries, dating back to the **Malla period**.

- It was traditionally a way to mark the **end of the rice harvesting season**, combining religious rituals with social gatherings and feasts.
