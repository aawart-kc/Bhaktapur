Yomari Purnima

Yomari Purnima: The Festival of Sweet Dumplings

Yomari Purnima is a traditional Newar festival celebrated in Bhaktapur, marking the end of the rice harvest. Devotees prepare **Yomari**, a steamed dumpling filled with sweet substances like molasses or sesame seeds, and offer them to gods as a gesture of gratitude. The festival blends religious devotion, culinary tradition, and community celebration.

1. Origin & Mythology

- Yomari Purnima honors the **agricultural bounty** and thanks deities for a successful harvest.

- According to legend, offering Yomari pleases **Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth**, ensuring prosperity and abundance for the community.


2. Historical Background

- The festival has been celebrated by the Newar community for centuries, dating back to the **Malla period**.

- It was traditionally a way to mark the **end of the rice harvesting season**, combining religious rituals with social gatherings and feasts.


3. When Is It Celebrated?

- Date: Celebrated on the **full moon day of the month of Thin (December–January)** in the Nepali calendar.

- The festival coincides with the **end of the rice harvest**, marking a time of abundance and sharing.


4. Key Locations

-Temples dedicated to Lakshmi and other local deities – for ritual offerings.

-Neighborhood squares and courtyards – for communal preparation and sharing of Yomari.

-Bhaktapur Durbar Square – central celebrations and cultural events sometimes take place here.


5. Major Rituals and Events

🥟 Preparation of Yomari

Families prepare **Yomari**, a dumpling made of rice flour dough filled with **molasses, sesame seeds, or chaku** (a traditional sweet). The dumplings are steamed and offered to deities before sharing among family members.

🛕 Temple Offerings

Devotees offer Yomari at local temples to please gods and ensure prosperity for the coming year.

🎶 Cultural Gatherings

Neighborhoods gather to **share Yomari, sing traditional songs, and perform rituals**, strengthening community ties.


6. Cultural Significance

-Thanksgiving for Harvest: The festival expresses gratitude for agricultural abundance.

-Community Bonding: Families and neighborhoods share food and participate in rituals together.

-Preservation of Tradition: Yomari Purnima maintains centuries-old **Newar culinary, religious, and social customs**.


7. Tourist Tips

-Try **freshly steamed Yomari** from local households or markets; it’s a seasonal specialty.

-Observe temple rituals respectfully and take photos only when permitted.

-Participate in community gatherings to experience Newar hospitality and traditional songs.


8. Hidden Gems

-Each neighborhood has its **own unique recipe** for Yomari, with variations in fillings and size.

-Some families use special clay molds to shape Yomari, a centuries-old craft.

-The festival highlights the Newar **connection between agriculture, spirituality, and culinary art**, offering insight into local life.
