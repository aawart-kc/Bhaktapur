Nritya-Vatsala Temple

Echoes of Grace and Devotion

Indrayani Temple, tucked near the bustling Pottery Square of Bhaktapur, is a revered shrine dedicated to the fierce and nurturing goddess Indrayani. Though often overshadowed by grander temples, this sacred space has been a quiet center of worship, feminine energy, and local celebration for generations.

Belonging to the group of Ashta Matrikas (Eight Mother Goddesses), Indrayani is deeply respected by the Newar community and especially worshipped by women seeking strength, protection, and blessings for family life.

1. Origins & Era: Believed to have been established during the Malla period, the temple’s modest but intricate architecture reflects traditional Newari artistry with carved wooden struts and brickwork rooted in tantric symbolism.

2. Earthquake Resilience: Damaged during the 2015 earthquake, the temple was rebuilt using time-honored construction methods, preserving its sacred essence and historical detail.

3. Indrayani Jatra Festival: Once a year, the temple comes alive with the Indrayani Jatra, a colorful celebration where the goddess is carried through the streets in a palanquin, accompanied by music, oil lamps, and ritual dances performed by devotees.

4. Spiritual Significance: Indrayani Devi is believed to be a powerful protector — especially for mothers and children. Locals pray to her for fertility, health, and emotional strength in times of hardship.

5. Hidden Secret: It is said that if you light a ghee lamp and whisper a heartfelt prayer within the inner sanctum, the goddess grants silent courage — especially to those carrying unseen burdens.

Fassi-Dega stands as a timeless symbol of Bhaktapur’s rich spiritual heritage. This unique temple, with its dome-shaped design, offers a peaceful sanctuary amidst the bustling Durbar Square. It has been a focal point for worship and community gatherings for centuries. The temple’s architecture blends traditional craftsmanship with influences that make it distinct in the cityscape. Pilgrims and visitors alike find solace here, drawn by the temple’s serene atmosphere and its role in local traditions. Throughout the year, Fassi-Dega remains a place where faith, culture, and history converge — quietly preserving the spirit of Bhaktapur for generations to come.
