    • Fassi-Dega Temple

    Echoes of Faith and Stone

    Fassi-Dega, nestled in the heart of Bhaktapur Durbar Square, is a serene and sacred Shiva temple that has witnessed centuries of devotion, festivals, and resilience. Its dome-shaped architecture sets it apart from the traditional pagoda temples in the area.

    Often called Silu Mahadev, this temple becomes the spiritual gateway for pilgrims during the Silu Jatra — a long walking journey to Gosaikunda that begins right here in Bhaktapur.

    1. Origins & Era: Built in the 17th century during the Malla period, the temple combines Indian shikhara-style architecture with intricate Newar wood carving and brick masonry, reflecting Bhaktapur’s golden age.

    2. Architectural Highlights: The temple’s tiered base, ornate torana (carved arch), and gilded pinnacle feature depictions of Hindu deities, mythical creatures, and tantric motifs.

    3. Earthquake Resilience: After being damaged in the 2015 earthquake, the temple was restored using traditional handmade bricks, sal wood beams, and lime mortar to maintain its authenticity.

    4. Silu Jatra Pilgrimage: The temple is the starting point of the Silu Jatra pilgrimage to Gosaikunda Lake, where devotees march for days while chanting and playing Newar musical instruments.

    5. Spiritual Significance: Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the temple is believed to protect Bhaktapur from disasters and misfortune, making it a vital spiritual center.

    6. Hidden Secret: Local belief says ringing the temple’s bronze bell with a pure heart sends your wish directly to Shiva’s sacred abode at Gosaikunda.

    7. Ritual Life: Daily worship includes morning aarti, incense offerings, and abhishek ceremonies where the Shiva lingam is bathed with milk, honey, and sacred water.

    8. Cultural Hub of Festivities: Besides Silu Jatra and Shivaratri, the temple comes alive during Teej, Maghe Sankranti, and Bala Chaturdashi, attracting crowds of devotees.

    9. Community Bond: The temple acts as a gathering place for local guthis, who manage rituals, music, and festival preparations, preserving traditions for future generations.

    10. Living Heritage: Beyond being a historical site, the temple remains an active center of faith, art, and cultural identity in Bhaktapur.

    Where is Fassi-Dega?

    Fassi-Dega lies right inside Bhaktapur Durbar Square, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Surrounded by other historical gems, it’s a peaceful spot often overlooked by fast-moving tourists.
