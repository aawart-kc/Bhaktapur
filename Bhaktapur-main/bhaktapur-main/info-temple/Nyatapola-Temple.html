Nyatapola Temple

Echoes of Strength and Symmetry

Nyatapola Temple, towering gracefully in Taumadhi Square of Bhaktapur, is a marvel of balance, devotion, and architectural brilliance. With its five majestic tiers, it stands as the tallest temple in Nepal — a timeless guardian of the city’s cultural pride.

Built not only as a shrine but as a symbol of strength, Nyatapola is dedicated to Siddhi Lakshmi, a tantric goddess whose power is said to surpass all other deities in the square.

1. Origins & Era: Erected in 1702 AD during the reign of King Bhupatindra Malla, the temple showcases the peak of Malla-era engineering and Newari craftsmanship.

2. Architectural Wonder: Its five-tiered pagoda structure rises 30 meters high, supported by massive stone guardians — wrestlers, elephants, lions, griffins, and goddesses — each pair more powerful than the one below.

3. Earthquake Endurance: Despite several major earthquakes, including 2015’s, Nyatapola has remained largely unscathed, a testimony to the genius of its traditional construction techniques.

4. Spiritual Significance: Siddhi Lakshmi is a fierce protector deity, worshipped for power, protection, and prosperity. Rituals held here are deeply rooted in tantric traditions.

5. Hidden Secret: Locals whisper that the strength of the temple lies not just in its foundation, but in the sacred balance of energies infused in its five levels — representing earth, water, fire, air, and ether.

Fassi-Dega stands as a timeless symbol of Bhaktapur’s rich spiritual heritage. This unique temple, with its dome-shaped design, offers a peaceful sanctuary amidst the bustling Durbar Square. It has been a focal point for worship and community gatherings for centuries. The temple’s architecture blends traditional craftsmanship with influences that make it distinct in the cityscape. Pilgrims and visitors alike find solace here, drawn by the temple’s serene atmosphere and its role in local traditions. Throughout the year, Fassi-Dega remains a place where faith, culture, and history converge — quietly preserving the spirit of Bhaktapur for generations to come.

Where is Nyatapola?

Nyatapola Temple stands tall in Taumadhi Square, just a short walk from Bhaktapur Durbar Square, within the UNESCO World Heritage Site.
